Basic Concept

Let XXX be n×kn \times kn×k matrix. Let nnn be the size of observation and kkk is the number of feature. PCA(Principal Component Analysis) produces transformed axes weights that explain variation in features.

Variation in feature

Let standardized XXX be ZZZ. Z′ZZ'ZZ′Z (k×kk \times kk×k) is a kind of variance-covariance matrix among features.
Note that Z′ZZ'ZZ′Z is real-valued symmetric matrix. This matrix can be decomposed by orthogonal diagonalization.

Z′Z=PΛP−1=PΛPT Z'Z = P \Lambda P^{-1} = P \Lambda P^T Z′Z=PΛP−1=PΛPT

Λ\LambdaΛ is a diagonal matrix that has eigenvalues on this diagonal elements by descending order. PPP is orthogonal matrix where iii-th column is the eigenvector of iii-th diagonal element.

Meaning

Eigenvalue

  • Each eigenvalue λ\lambdaλ represents the relative share in variance of ZZZ. That is, variation in XXX is explained by iii'th column of zzz up to λi∑iλi\frac{\lambda_i}{\sum_i \lambda_i}∑i​λi​λi​​.
    • Why is that? sum of diagonal entries of Z′ZZ'ZZ′Z is the sum of variance. Z′ZZ'ZZ′Z is symmetric matrix and all eigenvalues are positive. Also, sum of diagonal entries are equal to sum of eigenvalues!
  • We can choose PC by some criteria.
    • In most cases, one or two is suitable.

Eigenvector (Factor loading)

  • The iii-th column of PPP (k×1k \times 1k×1) is the weight for each feature in iii-th principal component of iii-th eigenvalue.
    • Each eigenvector is a direction of kkk-dimension vector space
    • Eigenvectors are mutually orthogonal.
